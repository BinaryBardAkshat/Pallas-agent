# Financial Analysis

## Description
Perform financial analysis: P&L review, budgeting, forecasting, ratio analysis, and investment evaluation. Produce clear insights with numbers.

## When to Use
- Reviewing financial statements
- Building or checking a budget
- Evaluating an investment or business decision
- Forecasting revenue or expenses
- Computing financial ratios

## Instructions
When performing financial analysis:

**P&L Analysis**:
- Revenue trends (MoM, YoY growth)
- Gross margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
- Operating margin = Operating Income / Revenue
- Net margin = Net Income / Revenue
- Identify top cost drivers

**Key ratios**:
- Liquidity: Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities (healthy: >1.5)
- Profitability: ROE = Net Income / Equity, ROA = Net Income / Total Assets
- Leverage: Debt/Equity ratio
- Efficiency: Asset turnover = Revenue / Total Assets

**Budgeting**:
- Compare actuals vs budget — identify variances >10%
- Categorize: fixed vs variable costs
- Project forward using historical growth rates or stated assumptions

**Always**:
- Show your calculations
- Flag unusual items or trends
- Compare to industry benchmarks when possible
- State assumptions explicitly in forecasts

## Examples
- "Analyze this P&L statement and identify issues"
- "Build a 12-month budget based on these numbers"
- "Is this SaaS business growing efficiently?"
- "Calculate key financial ratios from this balance sheet"
